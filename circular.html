Circular Questions for Team Dynamics

Circular questions come from systemic family therapy and are a powerful extension of Type 4 (Perspectives). Instead of asking someone about their own view, you ask person A to describe person B's perspective. This surfaces hidden dynamics, builds empathy, and reveals misalignment — without accusation.

They work because the person being asked has to genuinely step into someone else's shoes, and the person being described gets to hear how they're perceived.

Why they matter for product teams

  • They highlight the diversity of assumptions within a team
  • They explore interaction patterns and relational dynamics
  • They help the team develop systemic solutions rather than individual fixes
  • They build co-responsibility and team empowerment
